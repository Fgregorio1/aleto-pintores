## Why companies come back to us

Because the real cost of painting an office isn't the paint — it's every hour of stopped operations and every complaint from the team on Monday. The whole process is designed around that: out-of-hours surveys, night and weekend execution, odourless fast-drying materials, and individual protection of workstations and equipment. The office turns up painted; your operation doesn't notice. And the admin arrives on its own: invoice, certificates and H&S paperwork without you chasing anyone.

## What kind of spaces do we paint?

Offices and professional practices, retail and shops, restaurants and hospitality (washable, kitchen-grade coatings), clinics (antibacterial paints), gyms, academies, garages and warehouses with floor markings. Every use has its technical demand — grease, humidity, heavy traffic, disinfection — and there's a specific coating system for each. Part of our job is recommending the right one, not the most expensive one.

## How does a job run without stopping the business?

With a written plan before a single desk moves: which zones get painted each night or weekend, where materials are stored, who opens and closes, and one site contact reachable throughout. If anything deviates from the plan, you hear it from us before you discover it. Committed dates hold — in a shop, every day of delay is lost revenue, and that's exactly the standard we plan to.

## And the price, for internal budgeting?

Published so you can run numbers before asking for anything: €10–16 per m² in 2026, unsocial hours included. After the visit, a fixed itemised quote ready for your internal or accounting approval.
