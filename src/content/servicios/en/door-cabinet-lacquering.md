## Why lacquering transforms a Madrid flat

Most flats built between 1970 and 1995 came with dark varnished "sapelli" doors. Combined with smooth, freshly painted walls, white-lacquered doors are what makes those flats look genuinely renovated — at a fraction of replacement cost. It's the standard finishing move after [gotelé removal](/en/services/gotele-removal-wall-smoothing/), and we usually do both in a single coordinated job.

## What a professional lacquer actually involves

Lacquering is not "painting the door white". The process that lasts is: hardware off, sand and degrease, fill dents, **bonding primer** over the varnish (skip this and the finish peels), then two-three coats of polyurethane lacquer with fine sanding between coats. The difference shows against the light — and two years later, when a cheap job has chipped and a proper one hasn't.
