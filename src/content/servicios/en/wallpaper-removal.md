## Why is removal trickier than it looks?

Because the paper hides what's underneath, and removing it badly is expensive. A modern vinyl over sound walls comes off clean in hours; a 1980s paper with two layers and hardened glue can take the plaster with it if stripped dry. Our method starts with a test patch in a hidden spot to identify paper and layers, continues with steam and correct soak times, and finishes with the step almost everyone skips: washing off every trace of glue. A "paperless" wall is not the same as a wall ready to paint — the difference shows months later, when paint applied over glue starts to craze.

## What finish next — and what would we honestly recommend?

It depends on how the wall comes out, and we'll tell you straight even when the answer is the cheapest option. If the substrate is sound: targeted prep and **direct painting**, the most economical route. If repairs are extensive or you want a fine finish: **skim and paint** — the same process we use for [gotelé removal](/en/services/gotele-removal-wall-smoothing/). And if you love paper: **re-paper** a feature wall with a modern print, over a properly prepared base that will strip easily next time. The visit is free and the advice comes with no strings.
