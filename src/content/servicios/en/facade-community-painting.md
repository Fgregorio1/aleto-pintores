## What should a community demand before voting?

Three things: an honest diagnosis of the substrate (repainting is not the same as repairing structural cracks), an itemised quote the board can genuinely compare, and a realistic calendar that respects residents. Our quotes always separate repairs, access equipment and coating system, with a photo report of the current condition — the administrator presents it to the board as-is, and owners know exactly what they're voting on. We'll attend the junta to answer questions if useful.

## The right coating for each facade

The technical choice matters more than the brand. Sound render gets a high-durability smooth acrylic; micro-cracked surfaces get an elastic crack-bridging coating so the cracks don't reappear within months; brick and salt-affected substrates get breathable pliolite paint. A facade gets repainted every 10-15 years — the wrong system fails regardless of quality, and cheap material is the most expensive choice spread over those years. The community always receives the applied system's datasheet.

## What is the work like for the people living through it?

A facade is voted in a meeting but endured on the landing — we know. Advance notice in the lobby with the phased schedule, respectful hours, entrances protected and cleaned every evening, stairwells done in sections so passage never stops. The administrator has a direct site contact and weekly photo reports; residents get a site that advances without turning the building into a camp. Handover includes a photo report and a written warranty for the system applied.

Published and explained: €12–22 per m² in 2026, with the factors that move the range (substrate, height, access equipment) broken down in the [facade price guide](/en/prices/) — written so administrators and boards can compare offers properly.
