## How do you remove gotelé without a dust disaster?

Every gotelé horror story is about dust: weeks of breathing it and finding it in wardrobes months later. Our process is built to prevent exactly that. Each room is sealed floor-to-ceiling with plastic before the wall is touched; sanders run connected to industrial vacuum extraction rather than dry into the air; and no zone is closed without a full clean. The result: the flat stays livable every single day — most of our clients don't move out, and that's the honest test of whether a dust-control system works.

## What separates a professional smoothing from a mediocre one?

Raking light. A wall can look flat straight-on and ripple like water when light hits it at an angle — which is exactly how sunlight comes through a window. A serious job follows the full process: diagnosing the gotelé type (water-soluble or plastic paint, each with its own technique), two crossed coats of skim plaster laid by trowel, fine machine sanding, and a room-by-room check with a raking lamp before priming. We hand over with that test in front of you. Plaster also needs its drying times — distrust impossible schedules, because rushed smoothing stays in the wall forever.
