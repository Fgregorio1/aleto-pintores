# Aleto Pintores — SEO & AI Visibility Playbook (Madrid)

**The non-technical strategy document.** Read this first. The companion document (`02-seo-technical-execution.md`) tells whoever builds the site exactly how to execute.

---

## 1. The one-paragraph strategy

Aleto Pintores will win Madrid by becoming **the most trusted, most cited, most transparent painting company online in the city** — not the biggest advertiser. In 2026, customers find painters three ways: Google's local map results, classic Google search, and increasingly by asking AI assistants (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity) "who's a good painter in Madrid?". All three channels reward the same underlying things: a real business with a verifiable identity, an overwhelming volume of genuine reviews, content that actually answers what people ask (especially about **prices**), and independent third parties vouching for you. Everything in this plan builds those four assets.

## 2. How people actually search for painters in Madrid

Understanding the demand shapes everything else. Searches fall into five buckets:

1. **"Find me a painter" searches** — `pintores madrid`, `empresa de pintura madrid`, `pintor económico madrid`, `pintores cerca de mí`. High intent, brutal competition, won mostly through the **Google map pack** (the 3 businesses shown with the map), which is driven by your Google Business Profile, reviews, and proximity.
2. **Price research searches** — `cuánto cuesta pintar un piso de 80m2`, `precio pintar piso madrid`, `quitar gotelé precio`, `precio alisar paredes m2`. This is the **biggest content opportunity**. Spaniards research prices heavily before requesting quotes, and most painting companies hide their prices. Publishing honest, detailed prices is your unfair advantage — it wins Google rankings, wins trust, and is exactly the kind of concrete data AI models love to cite.
3. **Service-specific searches** — `quitar gotelé madrid`, `alisar paredes madrid`, `lacar puertas madrid`, `pintar fachada comunidad`, `pintura de oficinas madrid`. In Spain, **gotelé removal + wall smoothing is arguably a bigger commercial keyword than "painters" itself** — millions of Spanish flats built in the 70s–90s have gotelé and owners want it gone. This must be a flagship service page, not a bullet point.
4. **Neighborhood searches** — `pintores chamberí`, `pintores alcorcón`, `pintores las rozas`. Lower volume individually, but dozens of them, low competition, and they convert extremely well. This is where programmatic SEO comes in (explained in §7).
5. **English searches by expats** — `painters in Madrid`, `english speaking painter madrid`. Madrid has hundreds of thousands of expats, they pay well, they're underserved, and almost no competitor targets them. A handful of English pages can own this niche.

Commercial/B2B demand is its own track: **comunidades de propietarios** (homeowner associations repainting stairwells, garages, façades — recurring, high-ticket, decided by *administradores de fincas*), offices, retail, and landlords/property managers turning over rental flats. B2B deals rarely start from a Google search alone — but administrators *do* vet you online before signing, so the same trust assets close those deals too.

## 3. Who you're actually competing against

- **Lead marketplaces**: Habitissimo, Cronoshare, ProntoPro. They dominate price-related searches. Strategy: **join them** (cheap early leads + reviews + a citation/backlink for your business identity) while you **outrank them** on specific queries with better, more honest content. They write generic national price articles; you write Madrid-specific ones with real project photos and real invoices behind the numbers.
- **Established local painters** with dated websites and 50–300 Google reviews. Their weakness: thin content, no price transparency, no English, no AI-readiness. Their strength: years of reviews. You close the review gap with a systematic review engine (§6) — most of them collect reviews passively.
- **Directories and agencies' microsites** (`pintoresmadrid.eu`-style exact-match domains). Beatable with genuinely better content and a stronger review profile.

## 4. The four assets you're building (in priority order)

### Asset 1: Google Business Profile (GBP) — your single most important property
This is what feeds the map pack **and** it's the primary data source AI assistants consult when recommending local businesses. Treat it like a second website:
- Verified profile, category **"Pintor"** (primary) + secondary categories (empresa de pintura, contratista).
- A real Madrid address or a properly configured service-area business covering Madrid + the metro belt (Alcorcón, Móstoles, Getafe, Las Rozas, Pozuelo, Alcobendas…).
- Every service listed with descriptions and **prices or price ranges**.
- 3–5 new photos per week from real jobs (before/after is gold).
- A weekly GBP post (finished project, offer, tip).
- Q&A section pre-filled: post the 15 questions clients always ask, answer them yourself.
- Respond to **every** review within 24h, in a personal tone, mentioning the neighborhood and job type ("¡Gracias, María! Un placer alisar el salón de vuestro piso en Chamberí…") — this feeds keywords into your profile naturally.

### Asset 2: Reviews — your compounding moat
Volume, velocity, and recency of Google reviews are the strongest local ranking signal you directly control, and review content is what AI models quote when they say *why* they recommend a business. Target: **15–25 new Google reviews per month, every month** (system in §6). Secondary platforms matter for AI visibility: Habitissimo, Trustpilot, and Houzz reviews all get read by models.

### Asset 3: A content-rich website that answers everything
Not a brochure — a resource. Structure (technical detail in the other doc):
- One deep page per **service** (interior, gotelé+alisado, lacado de puertas, fachadas, comunidades, oficinas/locales, papel pintado removal, impermeabilización de terrazas).
- One page per **area** (Madrid districts + key metro towns), programmatically built but genuinely localized (§7).
- A **price guide hub**: "Cuánto cuesta pintar un piso en Madrid (precios 2026 reales)" and one price guide per service, updated twice a year, with an interactive calculator. This cluster alone can become the most-cited painting price resource in Madrid.
- **Project gallery** with real before/afters, tagged by neighborhood, service, and price band ("Piso 75m², Arganzuela, gotelé + alisado + pintura, 2.400€, 5 días"). Nothing converts like this, nothing feeds neighborhood pages like this, and it's evidence AI models can point to.
- English section: 3–5 pages for the expat market.

### Asset 4: Independent validation — mentions, links, press
Google's "prominence" factor and AI models' trust both come from third parties talking about you: directories, local press, supplier relationships, industry associations. This is the PR/backlink track (§8).

## 5. Being recommended by AI models — the part most competitors will miss

When someone asks ChatGPT or Claude "recomiéndame una empresa de pintura seria en Madrid," the model either (a) searches the web live and synthesizes the top results, or (b) recalls what it learned in training. You influence both the same way:

1. **Be unambiguous about who you are.** Same exact name, address, phone (NAP) everywhere on the internet. A clear "entity home" — your About page stating in plain language: what Aleto Pintores is, founded when, by whom, serving which areas, with which services, insured, with X reviews averaging Y. Models extract facts; give them clean facts to extract.
2. **Be present where models look.** Their live-search answers for local services draw from: Google Business Profiles, review platforms, "best painters in Madrid" listicles, directories, and local press. You want to appear in *many independent sources*, not just your own site. Getting into 3–4 "mejores pintores de Madrid" roundup articles is worth more for AI visibility than 20 generic backlinks.
3. **Publish citable, concrete data.** Models preferentially cite content with specific numbers, named sources, and clear structure. Your annual **"Estudio de precios de pintura en Madrid"** (real anonymized data from your own quotes: average €/m² by district, by service) is designed to be *the* source models cite for painting prices in Madrid — and every citation strengthens the association "painting in Madrid → Aleto Pintores."
4. **Keep your site technically open and readable to AI.** Allow AI crawlers, use structured data, answer questions directly at the top of pages, keep FAQ sections. (All specified in the technical doc.)
5. **Answer in the shape of the question.** Pages should open with a direct 2–3 sentence answer ("Pintar un piso de 80m² en Madrid cuesta entre 1.100€ y 1.700€ en 2026, dependiendo de…") before elaborating. That's what gets pulled into AI answers verbatim.
6. **Track it.** Once a month, ask ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity and Claude the 10 money questions ("mejor empresa de pintura en Madrid", "cuánto cuesta quitar gotelé en Madrid"…) and log whether Aleto appears. That's your AI-visibility KPI. Expect zero mentions for ~6 months, then a tipping point as reviews + citations accumulate.

**Honest expectation:** AI recommendations lag real-world reputation by months. You can't shortcut it; you *can* systematically build the inputs. The businesses AI recommends in 2027 are the ones building reviews and citations in 2026.

## 6. The review engine (non-negotiable operating routine)

1. **At job completion**, the crew lead does a walkthrough with the client. If satisfied, they say: "Nos ayudaría muchísimo una reseña en Google" and hand a card with a **QR code** straight to the review form.
2. **Same evening**, an automated WhatsApp: thanks + the review link. (WhatsApp, not email — this is Spain.)
3. **No response in 3 days** → one polite WhatsApp reminder. Never more than one.
4. **Every review answered within 24h**, personally, naming the service and neighborhood.
5. Monthly: pick the 2 happiest clients and ask them to also review on Habitissimo or Houzz.
6. **Never** buy reviews, never incentivize with discounts (violates Google TOS; a purge can wipe your profile and your rankings with it).

A 4.9★ profile adding 20 reviews/month will pass a stale 4.6★ profile with 200 total reviews within a year — recency and velocity matter that much.

## 7. Programmatic SEO — what it means here, and how not to screw it up

The idea: generate many similar pages from a template + data (e.g., `pintores-chamberi`, `pintores-getafe`… ×40). It absolutely makes sense for this business — **but Google actively penalizes "doorway pages"** (thin, near-duplicate local pages). The line between winning and getting torched is **unique local substance on every page**:

- Real projects done in (or near) that district, with photos and prices.
- Reviews from clients in that area.
- District-specific notes that only a local business would know (building stock: "en Chamberí predominan fincas clásicas con techos altos y molduras — el alisado y la pintura de techos se presupuestan distinto…"; in newer PAUs: smooth walls, different price point).
- District-level pricing (your own quote data).

**Phased rollout:** launch with 8–10 handcrafted pages for your priority areas. Add districts as you complete jobs there (a page earns the right to exist when you have local proof to show). Cap: roughly 21 Madrid districts + 15–20 metro towns. Never launch 40 empty template pages on day one.

The same programmatic logic applies to the **price guide cluster** (one guide per service × the calculator) and later to **"gotelé removal in {district}"**-type combinations — but only for combos with real search volume, and only with real data. Quality bar first, scale second.

## 8. PR, links, and citations — earning prominence

**Tier 1 — Citations (weeks 1–8):** consistent listings on Google, Bing Places, Apple Maps, Habitissimo, Cronoshare, ProntoPro, Houzz, Páginas Amarillas, QDQ, Yelp, Infobel, Cylex + Madrid-specific directories. Boring, mechanical, foundational — full checklist in the technical doc.

**Tier 2 — Earned mentions (months 3–12):**
- **The price study** (§5.3) pitched to Madrid media and real-estate/reform blogs — journalists constantly write "cuánto cuesta reformar" pieces and need current local data. One good study can earn 10–20 organic links + the AI-citation flywheel.
- **Get into roundups:** find every "mejores pintores de Madrid" listicle that ranks; email the authors with your review count, insurance, and photos. Some add you free; some are pay-to-play — a few of those are fine if the site is legit and ranks.
- **Local press hooks:** an immigrant-founder building a business in Madrid is a human-interest story local outlets and neighborhood papers (Gacetín Madrid, district blogs) actually run.
- **Suppliers & partners:** become a registered applicator/partner of paint brands (Titanlux, Bruguer, Jotun, Isaval) — many list partner companies on their sites (perfect links). Same with *administradores de fincas* associations and reform companies that subcontract painting.
- **Sponsor something small and local** (neighborhood sports club, fiesta del barrio) — cheap, real-world, linkable, photographable.

**What to refuse:** bought link packages, comment spam, PBNs, "guest posts" on irrelevant sites. In a niche this local, 30 real Madrid-relevant links beat 500 junk links — and junk risks the whole project.

## 9. Roadmap

**Months 0–2 — Foundation.** Company registered, domain + site live (core service pages, 8–10 area pages, first price guide), GBP verified and fully built out, all Tier-1 citations done, review engine running from job #1. *KPI: site indexed, GBP impressions growing, first 15–30 reviews.*

**Months 3–5 — Content engine.** Full price-guide cluster + calculator live; 2 supporting articles/month; project gallery growing weekly; English pages live; first roundup placements; start Habitissimo/Cronoshare for lead flow while organic ramps. *KPI: first page-1 rankings on long-tail price queries; 60–100 reviews; map-pack appearances in home district.*

**Months 6–9 — Authority.** Publish the price study + PR push; supplier/association links; expand area pages behind real jobs; refresh early content. *KPI: map pack top-3 in 3+ districts; page 1 for `quitar gotelé madrid`-class terms; first AI-assistant mentions.*

**Months 10–12 — Compounding.** 2026-refresh of all price content (freshness is a ranking and citation factor); scale programmatic pages to ~30 areas; consider the commercial/B2B content track (comunidades, oficinas) as its own cluster. *KPI: organic + GBP as the #1 lead source; consistent AI mentions on Madrid painting queries; `pintores madrid` page 1 in sight.*

Realism check: `pintores madrid` itself takes 12–24 months. The long tail (prices, gotelé, districts, English) starts paying in 3–6 months, and the map pack can pay even sooner in your home area. Meanwhile marketplaces + Google Local Services Ads (if available in Madrid) buy you leads so SEO can grow without starving the business.

## 10. Weekly operating rhythm (≈3–4 h/week + writing)

- **After every job:** photos (before/during/after) → gallery + GBP; review ask executed.
- **Weekly:** 1 GBP post; 3–5 GBP photos; answer all reviews/questions; 1 content piece (page, guide update, or project write-up).
- **Monthly:** AI-visibility check (10 prompts × 4 models, log results); Google Search Console review (what's growing, what's on page 2 and worth a push); competitor review-count snapshot; 2 outreach emails (roundups/press/partners).
- **Quarterly:** update all price pages with real quote data; technical audit; refresh the study.

## 12. The three things that make this plan work

1. **Radical price transparency.** Everyone hides prices; you publish them. It wins rankings, wins AI citations, and pre-qualifies your leads.
2. **Reviews as an operating system, not an afterthought.** 20/month, every month, answered.
3. **Proof over claims.** Every page anchored in real projects, real photos, real numbers, real neighborhoods. That's what Google's algorithms, AI models, and — conveniently — actual customers all reward.
